## Description

Pick your river. Pick your gear. DriftLogic builds your complete rig.

DriftLogic covers all of Steelhead Alley — ten Lake Erie tributaries across Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York — and wires each one to its live USGS gauge. The moment you open the app, current, water temperature, and clarity are filled in from real-time water data. Choose fly, spinning, or center-pin, and you get the full prescription: rod, reel, line, leader, tippet, five flies or lures matched to today's water, the reasoning behind every choice, and a local pro tip.

THE RIVERS
• Ohio: Vermilion, Rocky, Cuyahoga (lower), Chagrin, Grand, Ashtabula, Conneaut
• Pennsylvania: Elk Creek (via the Brandy Run indicator gauge locals watch), Walnut Creek
• New York: Cattaraugus Creek — with clarity read from its live turbidity sensor

HONEST DATA
Every gauge was audited station by station. Stale sensors are filtered out automatically, indicator gauges are labeled as exactly that, and rivers without a gauge say so — you set conditions by eye and DriftLogic does the rest. No fake numbers, ever.

THE FULL RIG, EVERY TIME
• Four targets: Steelhead, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye, Channel Catfish
• Three methods: Fly, Spinning, Center-Pin — including real center-pin float rigs
• Rod and reel suggestions at three price points ($ / $$ / $$$)
• Hatch matching for fly anglers: eggs, BWO, caddis, midge, stonefly
• "Why This Rig" — where fish hold and how flow, temp, and clarity change the bite
• Five hand-picked technique videos per species and method
• Customize any condition with one tap when you want to explore

Every recommendation comes from a decision engine validated across all 4,860 possible condition combinations. Works offline — live gauges are a bonus, not a requirement.

Built by Rocky River anglers, for the whole Alley.

## App Review notes (paste into the Review Notes field)
Self-contained fishing recommendation tool; no login. The live-conditions banner queries the public USGS water-data API for the selected river and fills the condition pickers automatically; offline, the app hides the banner and works fully manually. Two rivers (Walnut Creek PA, Ashtabula River OH) have no USGS station, which the app states plainly. Shop buttons open Amazon searches in the browser; Amazon links carry an affiliate tag and the app displays the Amazon Associates disclosure directly beneath them.
